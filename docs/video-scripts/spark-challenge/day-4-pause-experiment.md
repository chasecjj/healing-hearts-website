## HOOK (10-15s) -- Trisha

[warm, direct to camera]

Today might be the most important day of this challenge.

Not because of what you learn.

Because of what you practice.

## STORY (20-30s) -- Trisha

[leaning in slightly]

Here's what I see in my clients all the time.

Tension shows up -- a sharp word, a slammed door, a silence that cuts.

And suddenly you can't think.

Your heart is pounding.

You react before you even know what happened.

That's your Critter Brain.

It fired. It took the wheel.

And your CEO Brain -- the calm, whole-picture part of you -- went offline.

## JEFF SEGMENT (30-45s)

[JEFF ENTERS -- warm, standing beside Trisha]

[credible but relaxed, not lecturing]

So here's what's actually happening in your brain.

Your brain has an alarm system.

The moment it senses threat -- your system floods.

Fight. Freeze. Fawn.

That is Critter Brain doing its job.

It's not a flaw. It's protection.

But here's the thing -- you can train the pause.

There's a nerve -- the vagus nerve -- that runs from your brain all the way down.

It's your reset switch.

One slow breath activates it.

That breath is what lets your CEO Brain come back online.

[JEFF EXITS]

## THE TURN (15-20s) -- Trisha

[nodding, warm handoff]

Knowing the science is one thing.

Practicing the pause is everything.

When you catch yourself in that flood moment --

you don't have to react.

You can breathe, and you can choose.

## BRIDGE TO EMAIL (10-15s)

[smile, encouraging]

Your email today walks you through exactly how to try this.

The next time tension shows up -- you'll have a move.

## WARM CLOSE (5-10s)

[soft, sincere]

Day four. You're doing it.

See you tomorrow.
