## HOOK (10-15s)

When did you last tell your partner you're grateful for them --
on a random Tuesday?

Not an anniversary.
Not a birthday.
Just -- Tuesday.

## STORY (40-60s)

[leaning in]

I do this thing with Jeff.

Sometimes I'll just text him --
Hey, I've got lunch ready for you.
Can you come?

[beat]

Sometimes he can't.
He'll say, I've got a drug rep coming in,
or a meeting with a doctor.
And I say, okay.

But he always responds with,
"Thank you for thinking of me."

[softening]

And all day he carries that.
He told me, I'm so sad I didn't make it --
because it just meant that much.

[breath]

I have a client who did something similar.
He wanted to surprise his wife --
so he went and got a small thing of flowers.
She wasn't home, so he took a picture
and sent it to her.

He told me, "That's just doing things
I wouldn't have done before."

[beat]

And then there are the texts while you're apart --
sharing pictures, sending thoughts --
that kind of connection
gives a lot of peace and a lot of confidence.

[warm]

It's the day-to-day practices and disciplines.
That's what gratitude really is.

## THE TURN (15-20s)

[direct to camera]

Gratitude isn't an event.

It's a Tuesday afternoon interruption
that changes the whole day.

And you don't have to wait
for something big to happen
to say what you're already feeling.

## BRIDGE TO EMAIL (10-15s)

[warm smile]

Your inbox has today's exercise.
One text. Less than a minute.

It might be the best thing that happens to them all day.

## WARM CLOSE (5-10s)

Keep going. You're building something real.
See you tomorrow.
