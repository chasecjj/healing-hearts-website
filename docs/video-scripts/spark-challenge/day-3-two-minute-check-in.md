# Day 3 -- The 2-Minute Check-In

## Beat Sheet

---

### HOOK

[Look straight into camera. Warm, direct.]

How often do you ask about their day...

...and actually listen to the answer?

Not plan your response.
Not check your phone.
Just listen.

---

### STORY

[Lean in slightly. This one lands.]

I heard a story once that I've never forgotten.

Her son was asked what he wanted to be when he grew up.

And he said...

"A smartphone."

[Pause. Let it land.]

Because his parents always pay attention when the phone rings.

"There's a text -- come in.
When I come in, I'm asked to wait."

That little boy just wanted to be heard.

And here's what I know from sitting with couples every week --

that feeling does not go away when we grow up.

[Softer now.]

One of my clients described the moment her husband finally just...
listened.

She said, he just heard me.
He didn't fight back.

She talked about it like it was a gift.

---

### THE TURN

[Steady, grounded.]

Two minutes of real listening does more than an hour of half-attention.

And the only way to really give that...
is to be available.

Not fixed.
Not performing.
Just... there.

---

### BRIDGE TO EMAIL

[Warm smile.]

Today's email has one simple question to ask your partner --
and exactly how to hold space for the answer.

No fixing.
No advice.
Just two minutes of being fully present.

---

### WARM CLOSE

[Soft, genuine.]

That little boy wanted to be a smartphone.

Your partner just wants to be heard.

Check your email -- and give them that today.

---
