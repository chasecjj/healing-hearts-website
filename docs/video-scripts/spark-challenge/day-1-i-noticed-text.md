## HOOK (10-15s)

When was the last time you told your partner you noticed something --
not because they asked,
but just because you saw it?

## STORY (40-60s)

[leaning in]

I want to share something with you.

Jeff comes home for lunch.

[beat]

Now, that might not sound like much.
But for me -- how meaningful that is.

It's one of those things that,
when he puts in that little bit of extra effort,
it means a lot.

[softening]

And I watch couples discover this all the time.

One of my clients read her husband a letter.
She said, "I notice the small gestures.
The steady strength you offer.
The boys are so proud of you.
They see the difference in you -- and in our home."

[beat]

That's when I know something has shifted.

Because she started noticing things
that were never noticed before.
She told me, "I'm like -- oh, how special is this?"

That gets me excited.
Because those opportunity points were always there.
They just needed someone to look.

## THE TURN (15-20s)

[breath]

Here's what I know to be true.

Being noticed is being loved.

Most couples don't stop caring.
They just stop looking.

And when you start looking again --
everything changes.

And it might surprise both of you.
