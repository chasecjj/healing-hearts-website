# Spark Challenge Companion Video Scripts -- Design Spec

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Status:** Approved
**Related:** 2026-03-24-video-scripts-and-hosting-design.md (32 lesson scripts -- separate scope)
**Email pipeline:** 2026-03-24-email-copy-agent-design.md (AVNII framework for email rewrite follow-up)

## Overview

7 short companion videos (90-120 seconds each) for the 7-Day Spark Challenge lead magnet. These are filmed at Trisha's home with a teleprompter. The videos serve as **emotional anchors** -- they make the viewer *feel* something, while the daily email delivers the tactical exercise.

These are NOT the 32 lesson scripts (Phase B). These are short-form lead magnet content tied to the Be Healthy Expo deadline (April 10, 2026).

## Decisions

- **Role:** Emotional anchor. Video = feeling. Email = doing.
- **Length:** 90-120 seconds per video
- **Talent:** Trisha solo (Days 1, 2, 3, 5, 6) + Jeff cameo (Days 4, 7)
- **Filming:** Trisha's home, desk/couch setup (intimate, not studio), teleprompter
- **Source material:** Transcript library (34 sessions, 312K words) -- NOT the current email copy (which is AI impersonation)
- **Email improvement:** After video scripts are finalized, the 7 Spark Challenge emails should be rewritten through the AVNII email copy pipeline using the same transcript-sourced stories

## Script Structure

Every video follows this beat sheet:

| Beat | Duration | Purpose |
|------|----------|---------|
| **Hook** | 10-15s | Relatable question or moment that stops the scroll |
| **Story** | 40-60s | Personal Jeff+Trisha anecdote -- intimate, looking at camera |
| **The Turn** | 15-20s | Emotional pivot -- "here's what I learned" |
| **Bridge to Email** | 10-15s | "Check your inbox" + exercise tease |
| **Warm Close** | 5-10s | Encouragement, "See you tomorrow" (Day 7: soft course upsell) |

## Teleprompter Format

Scripts are written for the ear, not the eye:

- **Max 12 words per sentence** (natural speaking rhythm)
- **Contractions always** ("you're" not "you are", "don't" not "do not")
- **Pauses marked:** `[beat]` for a natural pause, `[breath]` for a longer one
- **Emotion cues:** `[leaning in]`, `[smiling]`, `[softening]` -- brief, not prescriptive
- **Stage directions:** `[JEFF ENTERS]`, `[JEFF EXITS]` for cameo transitions
- **Line breaks:** Every sentence on its own line for teleprompter readability
- **Word count target:** 225-300 words per script (natural speaking pace ~150 wpm)

## Voice Rules

- Trisha's teaching rhythm: **story -> feeling -> invitation**
- Proprietary framework names used exactly: Critter Brain, CEO Brain, 90-Second Wave
- No clinical language ("physiological stress response" -> "that tight feeling in your chest")
- No generic relationship advice without Healing Hearts framing
- Source from transcripts first. If transcript coverage is thin for a day, write in Trisha's voice profile but flag the AI-written percentage.
- Target: >=80% transcript-sourced content per script

## Day-by-Day Design

### Day 1 -- "The 'I Noticed' Text"

**Theme:** Observation as love language
**Talent:** Trisha solo

- **Hook:** "When was the last time you told your partner you noticed something -- not because they asked, but just because you saw it?"
- **Story direction:** Search transcripts for Trisha telling a story about small acts of service or noticing -- Jeff doing something quiet, or a client couple's breakthrough around feeling "seen"
- **Transcript search terms:** `notice`, `seen`, `small things`, `service`, `invisible`
- **Turn:** Being noticed = being loved. Most couples stop noticing.
- **Bridge:** "Your inbox has today's exercise -- it takes 30 seconds and it might surprise both of you."

### Day 2 -- "The Specific Spark Compliment"

**Theme:** Specificity signals attention
**Talent:** Trisha solo

- **Hook:** "We say 'you look nice' like it's a reflex. What happens when you get specific?"
- **Story direction:** Trisha sharing a moment when a specific compliment landed differently -- or a client story about generic vs. specific appreciation
- **Transcript search terms:** `compliment`, `specific`, `appreciate`, `noticed that you`, `paying attention`
- **Turn:** Specific means "I was paying attention." That's what your partner actually hears.
- **Bridge:** "Check your email -- I'll show you exactly how to do this."

### Day 3 -- "The 2-Minute Check-In"

**Theme:** Listening without fixing
**Talent:** Trisha solo

- **Hook:** "How often do you ask about their day and actually listen to the answer?"
- **Story direction:** Trisha on distracted listening, half-attention, or the difference when Jeff really listens. Maps to Module 4 (Active Listening).
- **Transcript search terms:** `listen`, `check in`, `how was your day`, `distracted`, `phone`, `present`
- **Turn:** Two minutes of real listening does more than an hour of half-attention.
- **Bridge:** "Today's email has the exact question and how to hold space for the answer."

### Day 4 -- "The Pause Experiment" (Jeff Cameo)

**Theme:** Self-regulation in conflict -- Critter Brain / CEO Brain preview
**Talent:** Trisha + Jeff (30-45s cameo)

- **Hook (Trisha):** "Today might be the most important day of this challenge."
- **Story (Trisha):** Sets up that tension is where couples lose each other -- a real moment where things escalated before anyone could think
- **Jeff enters (30-45s):** "As a family physician, here's what's actually happening in your body..." -- explains the 90-Second Wave simply. Warm, credible, brief. Not clinical.
- **Transcript search terms:** `critter brain`, `CEO brain`, `90 second`, `flood`, `pause`, `react`, `regulate`, `fight or flight`
- **Turn (Trisha):** "Knowing the science is one thing. Practicing the pause is everything."
- **Bridge:** "Your email walks you through how to try this the next time tension shows up."

**Jeff's segment guidelines:**
- Jeff speaks as a physician AND a husband, not a lecturer
- Language stays accessible: "your brain's alarm system" not "amygdala hijack"
- 30-45 seconds max -- he adds credibility then hands back to Trisha

### Day 5 -- "The Gratitude Text"

**Theme:** Gratitude as interruption of autopilot
**Talent:** Trisha solo

- **Hook:** "When did you last tell your partner you're grateful for them -- on a random Tuesday?"
- **Story direction:** Gratitude not as a Hallmark moment but a mid-day text that says "I see you." A real moment from their marriage or a client story.
- **Transcript search terms:** `grateful`, `thankful`, `appreciate`, `take for granted`, `text`, `surprise`
- **Turn:** Gratitude isn't an event. It's a Tuesday afternoon interruption that changes the whole day.
- **Bridge:** "Your inbox has today's exercise -- it takes less than a minute."

### Day 6 -- "The Memory Lane Moment"

**Theme:** Shared history as connection anchor
**Talent:** Trisha solo

- **Hook:** "Do you remember the moment you knew?"
- **Story direction:** Trisha's early relationship story -- the specific memory that made her certain. Must be a real story from transcripts, not fabricated.
- **Transcript search terms:** `remember when`, `first date`, `early`, `how we met`, `the moment`, `fell in love`, `beginning`
- **Turn:** Sharing that memory isn't nostalgia. It's reminding both of you why you chose this.
- **Bridge:** "Today's email helps you find your moment and share it with your partner."

### Day 7 -- "The Spark Conversation" (Jeff + Trisha Together)

**Theme:** Direct, vulnerable ask -- "What do you need from me?"
**Talent:** Trisha + Jeff together (full segment)

- **Hook (Trisha, scripted):** "This is the last day. And it's the one that can change everything."
- **Setup (Trisha, scripted, 15s):** Introduces the question: "What's one small thing I could do this week to make you feel loved?"
- **Live demo (30-45s, UNSCRIPTED):** Trisha asks Jeff the question on camera. Jeff answers honestly. This is NOT telepromptered. The authenticity IS the content.
- **Turn (Trisha, scripted):** Reflects on hearing his answer -- the vulnerability of asking and receiving
- **Bridge + soft upsell (scripted):** "If this week meant something to you, the full Healing Hearts course goes deeper into every single thing we practiced. I'd love to keep walking with you."
- **Close:** Warm sign-off, no pressure.

**Fallback for Day 7:** If a fully unscripted middle feels too risky, Jeff can know the question is coming but his answer remains his own. Trisha's setup and closing stay on teleprompter.

**Day 7 production scaffolding:**
- Plan for 2-3 takes. The first is usually the most authentic, but having options reduces pressure.
- Trisha has a scripted bridge line ready if Jeff's answer is brief: "I love that -- and that's exactly what this is about." She can riff from there naturally.
- Target 30-45 seconds for Jeff's response. If it runs long, Trisha has a natural interrupt cue -- she reaches for his hand and says "That's beautiful" to signal the transition back.
- If the emotional tone does not land on any take, Trisha can ask a follow-up: "What made you think of that?" to draw out more depth.

## Production Notes

### Filming Order Recommendation

Film the 5 Trisha solo videos first (Days 1, 2, 3, 5, 6) in one session. Then film Day 4 (Jeff cameo) and Day 7 (Jeff + Trisha together) in a second session. This keeps production efficient and lets Jeff's time be focused.

### Setup

- Desk or couch setup -- intimate, warm lighting, not a studio look
- Research confirms desk setup outperforms professional studio for course content (creates parasocial intimacy)
- Consistent background across all 7 videos for brand cohesion
- Teleprompter positioned near camera lens for natural eye contact
- **Aspect ratio:** 16:9 landscape for email/web embedding. Social media cuts (9:16 portrait) are a post-expo follow-up, not in scope.

### Equipment (per vault task)

- Teleprompter
- External mic (lapel or shotgun)
- Ring light or soft key light
- Camera or phone on tripod

## Script Generation Pipeline

### Step 1: Transcript Mining

For each day, search the 34-session transcript library using the specified search terms. Extract:
- Trisha's real stories matching the theme
- Her exact phrasing and metaphors
- Jeff anecdotes she references
- Client stories (anonymized) that illustrate the concept

**Search strategy:**
- Run each search term as a **separate query** (the search engine uses `websearch_to_tsquery` with a 20-result limit per query)
- Combine results across queries and de-duplicate
- If fewer than 3 usable excerpts are found for a day, expand: try related terms, search by speaker ("Trisha" + topic keyword), or broaden to adjacent themes
- **Thin coverage threshold:** If a script ends up <80% transcript-sourced after exhausting search, flag it and ask Trisha to provide a real story verbally during the teleprompter dry run. The story she tells on the spot replaces the AI-written placeholder.

### Step 2: Draft Assembly

Assemble scripts from transcript excerpts, fitting them into the beat sheet structure. Target >=80% transcript-sourced. Flag any AI-written bridges or transitions.

### Step 3: Voice Pass

Read each script aloud (or have AI simulate reading pace). Check:
- Does it fit in 90-120 seconds at natural pace (~150 wpm)?
- Does it sound like Trisha talking to a friend, not reading an essay?
- Are there any stumble points (tongue-twisters, unnatural phrasing)?
- Are emotion cues natural, not over-directed?

### Step 4: Trisha Review

All 7 scripts go to Trisha for read-through and approval. She may:
- Swap stories for ones she'd rather tell
- Adjust phrasing to match how she'd actually say it
- Add or remove emotion cues
- Approve or request re-draft

### Step 4.5: Teleprompter Dry Run

Before final formatting, Trisha reads one script aloud (Day 1 recommended -- simplest structure) to:
- Calibrate teleprompter scroll speed to her natural pace
- Catch any phrasing that feels unnatural when spoken
- Fill in any thin-coverage gaps with her own stories on the spot
- Confirm the beat sheet timing lands in 90-120 seconds

### Step 5: Teleprompter Formatting

Convert approved scripts to teleprompter-ready format:
- One sentence per line
- Large font, high contrast (teleprompter app determines exact font size -- calibrate during dry run)
- Stage directions in brackets
- Scroll speed calibrated to Trisha's natural pace (set during dry run)
- Output format: plain text files (`.txt`) -- compatible with all teleprompter apps

## Delivery (Recommendation)

Videos can be delivered via:
1. **YouTube Unlisted** links embedded in the daily emails (simplest, no code changes)
2. **Added to the Spark Challenge landing page** after signup (requires minor page update)
3. **Dedicated /spark-challenge/day-N pages** (most work, best UX)

**Recommendation for expo deadline:** Option 1 (YouTube Unlisted). Zero code changes. Add the link to the top of each email template after recording. Upgrade to Option 2 or 3 post-expo.

## Email Rewrite (Follow-Up)

After video scripts are finalized and transcript stories are identified, the 7 Spark Challenge emails should be rewritten through the AVNII email copy pipeline:
- Replace AI-impersonated stories with the same transcript-sourced stories used in the videos
- Maintain story continuity: video tells the emotional version, email expands with exercise details
- Apply AVNII stages: Acknowledge -> Validate -> Normalize -> Illuminate -> Invite
- Run anti-shame audit and deliverability checks

This is a separate task that follows video script completion.

## Post-Expo Follow-Ups (Out of Scope)

- Closed captions / subtitles (YouTube auto-captions as interim, burned-in captions for accessibility)
- 9:16 portrait social media cuts for Instagram Reels / TikTok
- Migration from YouTube Unlisted to Mux or Cloudflare Stream (see hosting comparison research)
